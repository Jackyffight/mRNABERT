# ADR 0004: Route Candidates By Evidence Cost, Not One Model Threshold

Status: accepted, 2026-07-15

Extends ADR 0003 after the first 384-candidate Stage 3 search run.

## Context

The three-protein Mock run produced 9 higher-confidence, 18 mixed-confidence, and
357 low-confidence ESMFold2-Fast structures. Eight of the nine higher-confidence
records were newly enumerated B5 boundary variants; no fusion entered that band.
Selecting only higher and mixed confidence would therefore reduce 384 candidates to
27 and strongly bias the program toward short B5 truncations before immune evidence,
developability evidence, product context, or experimental evidence is considered.

ESMFold2 global confidence is useful structural evidence, but it is not the final
objective. Long, flexible, multi-domain, and linker-containing constructs may receive
lower global pTM even when individual components remain usable. Conversely, a short
high-confidence structure does not establish immune protection or manufacturability.

## Decision

Candidate routing follows evidence cost and complementarity:

1. All 384 Stage 3 candidates proceed through Stage 4 immune assessment and Stage 5
   sequence/developability assessment. Missing external evidence remains explicit;
   it is never converted into a favorable score.
   The complete 2,276-record Stage 2 batch remains archived for lineage, while the
   verified Stage 3 handoff is authoritative for this 384-record active set.
2. No candidate is removed from lineage or future reconsideration solely because its
   Stage 3 confidence band is low.
3. Expensive follow-up uses three versioned lanes:
   - **priority:** all higher- and mixed-confidence candidates, currently 27;
   - **diversity rescue:** a deterministic subset of low-confidence candidates that
     preserves source/manual controls, antigen composition, architecture, order,
     linker family, and candidates supported by complementary Stage 4/5 evidence;
   - **archive:** remaining candidates retained with complete evidence and lineage,
     but not automatically allocated the next expensive-compute budget.
4. GPU structure reruns, ESM3/ProteinMPNN redesign, and wet-lab shortlists consume a
   separate declared budget and a checksum-bound routing manifest.
5. Lane assignment is generated by versioned code from immutable evidence. An LLM
   may explain or challenge the assignment but cannot silently change it.

## Consequences

Stage 4/5 process more records, but their CPU and sequence-level cost is small relative
to structure generation and experiments. The workflow avoids premature single-model
selection while still controlling expensive downstream work. Fusion architectures
remain represented long enough for component confidence, junction liabilities,
immune evidence, and developability evidence to influence the decision.

The current 27-record priority count is run-specific, not a permanent quota. The
diversity-rescue size and expensive-compute budgets must be added as versioned policy
before the next costly model round; they must not be chosen ad hoc from the report.

The first full active-set Stage 4/5 execution subsequently completed for all 384
candidates. This validates the all-candidate low-cost route. It does not alter the
decision that Stage 6 generation and later expensive model calls require an explicit
priority/diversity/archive routing manifest.
